British Parliamentary

The international gold standard
Teams
4 × 2
Prep Time
15m
Speech
7 min
Motion
Impromptu

Four teams compete in a pie chart structure: Opening Government, Opening Opposition, Closing Government, and Closing Opposition.

Points of Information (POIs) allowed between 1st and 6th minute. Teams must extend arguments, not merely repeat them. Ranking system from 1st to 4th place.
Opening teams should set up clear definitions and arguments. Closing teams must provide meaningful extensions or refinements while attacking other team’s case.

Emphasizes moral philosophy, logic, and value systems. Debaters clash over competing ethical frameworks rather than just policy mechanisms.

AC (6m), Cross-X (3m), NC (7m), Cross-X (3m), 1AR (4m), NR (6m), 2AR (3m). Heavy focus on Value and Value Criterion clash.

Public Forum

Current events for everyone
Teams
2 × 2
Topic
Monthly
Speech
4 min

Evidence

Designed to be understandable to lay audiences. Topics change monthly and focus on current events news stories.

  • Coin flip determines pro/con
  • Grand Crossfire debate
  • Summary and Final Focus speeches
  • Evidence-based but accessible

Policy Debate

The research intensive
Speed
High
Research
Yearly
Speeches
8-9m
Focus
Plan

The oldest format. Affirmative presents a specific policy plan; Negative argues against it using disadvantages, counterplans, or kritiks.

Includes Kritiks (challenging underlying assumptions), Counterplans (alternative policies), and T debates (Topicality). Extremely evidence-heavy with “cards” reading.
